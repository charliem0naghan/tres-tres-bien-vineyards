Foundation

Wine has always been about community. The Vines Foundation is our commitment to the people and the land that make Tres Très Bien possible.

Our Commitment

Beyond the
bottle

When the three founding families of Tres Très Bien chose to plant vines in Tunuyán, they understood they were not simply entering the wine business. They were entering into a relationship with a place — with its soils, its climate, its history, and above all its people. The Vines Foundation is the formal expression of that relationship.

The Uco Valley is one of Argentina's most exciting wine regions, but the communities that live and work within it face real challenges. The men and women who tend the vines, operate the irrigation systems, and carry out the physical labour of farming are the foundation on which every bottle of wine here rests. Their children deserve educational opportunity. Their farming practices deserve support toward greater sustainability. Their communities deserve investment.

A portion of every bottle of Tres Très Bien wine sold goes directly to the Foundation. The allocation is not symbolic — it is material, and it grows as our production grows. This is the mechanism we have chosen to ensure that the value we derive from this landscape flows back into it.

Foundation Programs

Three areas of focus

The Vines Foundation concentrates its work in three interconnected areas, all rooted in the communities of Tunuyán and the broader Uco Valley.

Education

The children of vineyard workers deserve access to the same educational opportunities as any child. The Foundation supports schooling, materials, and after-school programmes for families employed in viticulture and related agricultural work across the Tunuyán department.

We fund school supplies, provide scholarships for secondary and tertiary education, and support a reading and literacy programme in partnership with local schools in Tunuyán township.

Education for vineyard families · Tunuyán Township

Sustainable Agriculture

The long-term health of the Uco Valley depends on farming practices that work with the land rather than against it. The Foundation supports local growers — particularly small, family-operated farms — in transitioning toward more sustainable viticulture and agriculture.

This includes support for organic certification, water conservation practices, soil health improvement, and the reduction of chemical inputs across smallholder farms in the Los Chacayes corridor.

Sustainable farming · Water conservation · Soil health

Community

The Vines Foundation believes that a thriving wine region depends on thriving communities. Beyond education and agriculture, the Foundation invests in community infrastructure and cultural initiatives in Tunuyán — supporting local sports clubs, community events, and cultural programmes that strengthen the social fabric of the valley.

We are committed to being present in the community year-round, not only at harvest. Our families visit regularly, and the Foundation's work is guided by the community's own identified needs.

Why It Matters

Wine is made by
human hands

The language used to describe wine often focuses on the natural — the soil, the climate, the vine. But wine is not made by nature alone. It is made by people: the workers who prune the vines in winter and pick the grapes in autumn, the cellar hands who monitor fermentation through the night, the families who have lived and worked in Tunuyán for generations before any of our three founding families had ever heard of the Uco Valley.

Every bottle of Tres Très Bien wine carries the invisible labour of that community. The Vines Foundation is our attempt to make that relationship visible, and to ensure that the exchange is genuinely reciprocal — not simply extractive, but generative.

We do not pretend that a charitable foundation resolves every complexity of a global wine industry. But we believe that the choice to act — to commit a portion of every sale to the people and the land that make the wine possible — is the right choice, made for the right reasons. It is also, simply, the kind of winery we want to be.
