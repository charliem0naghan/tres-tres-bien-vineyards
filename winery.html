Our Story

A name born from
three perfect numbers

In 2016, a trip to the Uco Valley changed everything. Captivated by the altitude, the soil, and the wines it was producing, our founders made a decision — purchase land, grow grapes, and make wonderful wine in this special place.

The name Tres Très Bien is a quiet story in itself. Three families, each with three children. American ownership, French grape varieties, Argentine terroir. It is a name that spans languages — Spanish, French, English — to say simply: three is very good, indeed.

Our lots in Los Chacayes, Tunuyán are harvested by hand. Fermented in first-use French oak. Bottled in small runs of just 300 bottles per varietal. The result is wine that carries the fingerprint of a particular place, a particular year, and three particular families.

The Terroir

The Vines
of Mendoza

Nestled in the Los Chacayes sub-zone of Tunuyán, the Uco Valley sits at the foot of the Andes at elevations exceeding 1,100 metres. The combination of intense solar radiation, cool nights, and alluvial soils creates ideal conditions for wines of extraordinary depth and structure.

French varietals — Cabernet Franc, Malbec, Chardonnay — have found a second home here. In this soil they develop a New World intensity while retaining the elegance of their European origins.

Red Wine

Malbec

2021 · 2022 · 2023 Vintages

High-altitude Malbec grown in the cool Uco Valley, far removed from the valley-floor style. Expect violet aromatics, plush structure, and a long, mineral finish.

Blend: 90% Malbec / 10% Cabernet Franc
Production: 300 bottles per vintage
Origin: Los Chacayes · Tunuyán · Uco Valley

Giving Back

The Vines
Foundation

From the beginning, Tres Très Bien was built on the belief that a vineyard should be more than a place that makes wine. The Vines Foundation is our commitment to the communities — in Argentina and beyond — that make this work possible.

A portion of every bottle sold supports the Foundation's programs. Through it we invest in education, local agriculture, and sustainable vineyard practices that honour the land and the people who tend it.
