The Estate

Los Chacayes, Tunuyán. The land that three families chose in 2016 — and have been learning from ever since.

The Property

A place of
particular beauty

To stand in the vineyard at Los Chacayes on a clear winter morning is to understand immediately why three families chose this place above all others. The Andes rise to the west in a wall of snow-capped granite so close they seem touchable. The sky above is the particular deep blue that only high altitude and low humidity can produce. The air carries a sharpness that is almost sweet — thin, clean, smelling faintly of stone and sage.

The estate sits in the Tunuyán sub-zone of the Uco Valley, in a locality called Los Chacayes — a name that refers to the quebradas, the gorges, that cut down from the mountains toward the valley floor. The soils here are alluvial and ancient, laid down by glacial outwash over thousands of years: a surface of gravel and coarse sand over a deeper subsoil of clay and rock. Drainage is excellent. Irrigation is necessary, but precise — the vines receive only what they need, drawn from the Andean snowmelt that feeds the irrigation channels of Tunuyán.

Our parcels are compact — deliberately so. Small plots allow us to know every vine, to see changes before they become problems, to make decisions at the individual row level rather than the hectare level. This intimacy with the land is not incidental to our philosophy. It is the philosophy.

Three Pillars

What defines the estate

The Land

Los Chacayes represents some of the most precisely defined terroir in the Uco Valley. The combination of altitude, soil composition, and Andean proximity produces microclimatic conditions found nowhere else in Argentina — or the world.

1,100m+ · Gravel-loam · Andean alluvial soils

The Families

Three American families — the Monaghans, the Loeckes, and Jeff Warren — united by friendship, by a shared conviction about this valley, and by the decision to commit to something genuinely long-term. Each family brings three children to the venture: nine young people who will inherit what we are building here.

3 families · 9 children · Est. 2016

The Vision

Tres Très Bien was never conceived as a commercial wine operation in the conventional sense. It was conceived as a project of care — for the land, for the local community, for the craft of winemaking, and for the next generation who will carry the estate forward. Wine is the expression of that care.

300 bottles per varietal · 100% estate fruit · Since 2016
The Founding Story · 2016
A journey that
changed everything
The founding purchase in 2016 followed a trip to the Uco Valley that left the families convinced: this landscape, this altitude, this soil — it was producing wines of a quality and character that demanded closer involvement. They did not come to Mendoza as investors. They came as believers.
"We didn't buy land. We made a commitment."

The Founding

Three families,
one decision

The Monaghans, the Loeckes, and Jeff Warren came to the Uco Valley separately and arrived at the same conclusion together: this was a place where something extraordinary was possible, and they wanted to be part of making it. The decision to purchase land in Los Chacayes was not the conclusion of a business plan — it was the consequence of falling in love.

The name they gave the vineyard — Tres Très Bien — is the story in miniature. Three families. Three children in each. Three languages to say the same thing: very good, indeed. The Spanish tres, the French très bien, the English implied in the name's obvious delight. It is a name that carries its meaning lightly and precisely.

Since 2016, each family has remained deeply involved in the estate. They visit, they taste, they argue — productively — about direction and decisions. The winemaking team in Mendoza carries out the vision, but the vision belongs to all of them equally. This is the character of the estate: shared ownership, shared responsibility, shared pride.

"Tres Très Bien is not what we built. It is what we are building — together, season by season."
